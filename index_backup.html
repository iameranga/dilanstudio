  • My Bio

    Dilan Prasanna Thisera

    Dilan Prasanna Thisera Was born in 6th June 1984 at Colombo, Sri Lanka. In childhood Dilan Prasanna was a peaceful and active child. First he attended President College, Homagama. Dilan always did school works and other activities at school. He was interested drawing, paintings, dancing, also music. In Grade 5 He won the scholarship.
    After he passed Grade 5 Scholarship Dilan Prasanna went to Dharamapala Vidyalaya, Pannipitiya. At school he enjoyed activities and study well with his friends. In that time Dilan very interested in Photography. In Grade 9, he joined School Photographic society as Charter Member. Then Dilan started photography as his main hobby. He did nature, life, places and people photography with his little camera that time. Always he tried to make photos with his new angles.
    Dilan was a prefect and active member of school traffic unit. In School Photographic Society Dilan held member, and assistant Organizer posts. Then he was Chief Organizer in the Society 2003. This time he organized an All Island Exhibition with other Sri Lankan school photographic societies. Dilan won the 1st place for his one of Photograph in this exhibition. He named it as “Dancer with Drummer”. Dilan joined to Leo Club of Homagama Central, The International Association of Lions Clubs, District 306 C2, Sri Lanka as a charter board of directors 2006.

    After Ordinary Level and during the Advance Level he attended to a 3 years Diploma in Photography in The National Photographic Art Society of Sri Lanka in 2001. Then he started his own Studio named “Dilan Studio”

  • Leo Club of Homagama Central

    Dilan Prasanna Thisera was persident of Leo Culb

    Leo clubs are open to young men and women of good character, ages 12 to 28, who are dedicated to making a difference in their communities. Each Leo clubs is sponsored by a lions club. Leo Club members elect their own officers, Schedule their own meetings and select their own fundraisers and service projects.
    Popular service initiatives among Leo clubs include protection of the environment; community improvement; Drug abuse prevention; Health education; Educational assistance; Social services; and citizenship promotion.
    The social aspects of the Leo club program provide opportunities to meet other young people who share common interests, values and goals. The international scope of Leo clubs provides for interaction at regional Leo forums, he lions international convention, “Club Twinning” or pen pal. In addition, Leos can apply for participation in the Lions international youth exchange and youth camp programs.
    The Leo Club program motto is: Leadership, Experience, and Opportunity.
